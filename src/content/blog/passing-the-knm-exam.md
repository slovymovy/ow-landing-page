**KNM**, or *Kennis van de Nederlandse Maatschappij* (Knowledge of Dutch Society), is one part of your *Inburgering* process, along with four other sections: Reading, Listening, Writing, and, of course, the dreaded Speaking. I successfully passed my *Inburgering* a few months ago and wanted to leave a few tips here. I hope they help you!

## Format and recent changes

The test consists of **40 questions** covering **8 topics** about the Netherlands. As of 2026, you must answer at least **28 questions correctly** to pass. All questions are single-choice (only one answer is correct) — you never have to select more than one option.

In July 2025, several changes were introduced. The authors decided to remove subjectivity from the questions, focusing strictly on factual knowledge:

> "Also, the questions are more focused on knowledge instead of behavior." — [Rijksoverheid](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2025/06/30/inburgeringsexamen-vernieuwd-extra-aandacht-voor-zelfbeschikking-vrouwen-en-kennis-holocaust)

## When to prepare and take the exam

I believe it's better to save KNM for the end of your preparation process. Why?

1. All questions are in Dutch. As my friend says: "If you can understand the sense of the question, you are halfway to success." You need a basic vocabulary, which you build naturally throughout your entire study process.
2. While you study for the other sections, you'll read and listen to material about the Netherlands and its people — their habits, holidays, and lifestyle. This "passive" learning is a huge help for the KNM test.

## Study materials

I used three sources to prepare:

- **TaalCompleet book.** This provides brief but comprehensive information divided into the eight main chapters. I read it two or three times.
- **YouTube — search for KNM.** Not all KNM videos are equally good; some are painful to watch due to robotic voices or a very slow pace. My favorite — and the only playlist I needed — is from MarianNt2: [KNM Playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLALQ1_VZvx3jMylkiZDQhZ0ukIQSpvVv_). It's entirely in Dutch, but don't be afraid.
- **Official practice exams.** Use the practice tests on the official website: [inburgeren.nl](https://www.inburgeren.nl/examen-doen/oefenen.jsp).

One note: I don't think materials created before July 2025 are outdated, since the facts about the Netherlands remain the same.

## Tips during the exam

- **Don't rush.** You'll have plenty of time.
- **Don't panic.** If you don't have an answer immediately, skip the question and come back to it later.
- **Use common sense.** If you're stuck, lean on common sense and your life experience. Try remembering how things were done in your home country — that may also help.
