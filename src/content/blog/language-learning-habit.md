Why are we talking about habits? I believe they carry us to our goals. I believe in small and consistent steps, and I believe in the compound effect.

Below are the principles I follow, and then how they actually look in my week.

## The principles I follow

- Consistency. Just show up. It's like they say about the gym: just put your sports clothes on. You'll find your pace.
- Don't expect immediate results. This is a marathon. Appreciate small wins.
- Find a time in the day when you are 1) productive and 2) uninterrupted, and schedule the most important piece there.
- Build this new habit on top of an existing one — this is called habit stacking — so you don't have to make an effort figuring out when to squeeze in your learning sessions. My cue is coffee: I get up, make my first coffee, and bring my books to the kitchen.
- When I have no energy or a hangover, I skip all the learning and don't punish myself.
- When I have a long break, I don't punish myself either. I just commit that I'll get back on track when I feel right.
- Yes, you can also read *Atomic Habits* for inspiration, if you haven't already. Or watch one podcast with the author and you'll get the idea.

## My daily language-learning routine

- Every morning at about 8am: I do my homework and self-study through exercises. These are mainly reading and writing. Morning is the peak of productivity for me, and my job hasn't started yet. A person can effectively focus on studying for no longer than 90 minutes, so I usually do 50–60 minutes.
- Listening: I listen to podcasts when I either walk or clean the house. This way I can focus on listening and try not to get distracted.
- Lessons with a tutor two times a week, which is more speaking practice and a check of my homework. This is non-negotiable since I pay for it.
- Vocabulary drills with [OpenWords](/) either on a lunch break or before going to sleep. Sometimes on a train or a bus. No more than 30 minutes, so I don't lose focus. I can be not on top of my form, but I should be focused. No double screening, no background series.

I've been doing this for 1 year. I can finally see the results, since I'm not afraid to start speaking with people. I know I'm not even in the middle of the journey. And since I have these established routines, I'm not afraid of it. I know I'll be all right.

I wish you all to find your rhythm and small occasions to celebrate your successes.
